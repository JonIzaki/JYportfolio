Intel work experience

About my role as a validation engineer at Intel Jerusalem

Quick overview

I've started my engineering career at Intel working as a Validation Engineer where I design and implement test suites for embedded systems as part of the content protection team.
As it was my entry point to the engineering world I encountered the agile vs. waterfall discussions, learned about the engineering team structure and continuous integration processes which my team was a key part of.
The main focus of my work was analyzing communication protocols, mainly HDCP and coming up with a test plan that included regression and end to end tests. I professionalized in analyzing systems for weaknesses, creating high test coverage plans and implementing those plans using C#.
During this time I developed hundreds of automated tests, and maintained clean documentation to be used by the regression and other QA members. I've found unique and critical bugs during that time and worked alongside the developers to fix them in the C code base.
My experience at Intel taught me good QA practices in an agile process, as well as the beauty found in bug hunting.
